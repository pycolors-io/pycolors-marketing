## Secure delivery after purchase

Starter Free gives you a product surface.

Starter Pro gives you a **real paid product flow** with controlled delivery after purchase.

That matters because a digital product is not only about checkout.

It is also about what happens immediately after payment:

- access is confirmed
- delivery is controlled
- the buyer receives the product
- the asset is not publicly exposed
- fulfillment does not rely on manual work

## Delivery lifecycle

<Steps>
  <Step>
    ### Buyer completes checkout

    Payment happens through Stripe Checkout.

    Checkout is the commercial action, but the browser redirect alone should not be treated as the source of truth.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Webhook confirms the purchase

    Stripe sends the confirmed event to your backend.

```txt title="stripe-events.txt"
checkout.session.completed
payment_intent.succeeded
invoice.payment_succeeded
```

    The backend verifies the event and updates the internal purchase or access state.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Access is granted

    The app associates the purchase with the buyer, customer, or user account.

    This is where billing, auth, and backend ownership connect.
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Download token is created

    A delivery token can be created for controlled access.

```ts title="delivery-token.ts"
const token = await createDownloadToken({
  userId,
  productId: "starter-pro",
  expiresIn: "24h",
});
```
  </Step>

  <Step>
    ### Backend serves the asset

    The buyer requests the download through your backend.

    The backend validates access and streams or redirects to a private file source without exposing a permanent public URL.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout title="Production rule">
  Redirects are UX. Webhooks confirm purchase. Backend access controls delivery.
</Callout>

### Why this matters

Delivery is the final step in the buyer journey.

If delivery is slow, manual, fragile, or unclear, the purchase experience feels less trustworthy.

A strong delivery system helps you:

- reduce support friction
- avoid manual fulfillment work
- protect paid assets
- improve buyer confidence
- scale sales without operational drag

<Callout type="info" title="Buyer trust principle">
  The buyer should never wonder what happens after payment.
  Confirmation and access should feel immediate, clear, and controlled.
</Callout>

### Implementation checklist

Before treating delivery as production-ready, validate:

- checkout completion
- webhook confirmation
- purchase-to-access mapping
- private asset storage
- token creation
- token expiration
- token validation
- backend download route
- unauthorized access behavior
- expired token behavior
- retry path for interrupted sessions
- buyer-facing confirmation copy

<Callout title="Delivery validation principle">
  A paid product is not complete when checkout works.
  It is complete when the buyer reliably receives controlled access.
</Callout>

## Common questions

<Accordions type="single">
  <Accordion title="Why not just email a public download link?">
    Public links are easy to share, hard to revoke, and weak for paid product delivery. Backend-controlled access gives you a stronger commercial foundation.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why are webhooks part of delivery?">
    Because browser redirects can be interrupted or faked at the UX level. Webhooks confirm the payment state before access is granted.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can delivery work without user accounts?">
    It can, but accounts make access recovery, support, customer history, and repeat access much cleaner. For a SaaS product, auth and delivery should usually work together.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I replace the storage provider?">
    Yes. Keep the same boundary: private storage, backend validation, controlled delivery, and no permanent public access to paid assets.
  </Accordion>
</Accordions>
